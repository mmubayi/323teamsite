Phase One

Project Pitch

The question we are addressing through this problem is as follows: How might we use stories as a way to help facilitate conversations about difficult topics and opposing viewpoints?

Our web application aims to overcome echo chambers and political and ideological distancing by allowing users to address difficult topics through storytelling. We are designing this application with the assumption that our users are Wellesley College students. Login will then be limited to Wellesley students; however, they will remain anonymous whilst using the app. The anonymity prevents biases when reading other people’s stories, as well as a greater feeling of safety when writing and sharing personal stories. Users may take two main actions on the platform: Ask a question or post a story in response to a question. The stories that are written in response will potentially be subject to a minimum character count in order to give users time to consider their thoughts more fully before posting.

The two papers we will reference while building this platform are The Sociology of Storytelling and Political Blend. The Sociology of Storytelling addresses how this narrative form is used to communicate, specifically in the realm of politics. Political Blend discusses the concepts of echo chambers with the overall goal of creating an application that combats political echo chambers through meeting people with opposing views. With Political Blend’s approach as a starting point, we added the Sociology of Storytelling on top of those idea to approach combatting echo chambers with a new approach.

Phase Two

Authentication & Data Organisation

The social system we are building will contain the main components of a social network site: profiles, lists of users (categories), and an activity feed. In this project phase, we setup these main building blocks using Firebase, created an ER diagram to represent the relationships that will exist within our system, and sketched out designs for the main three components necessary for our social system.

Entity Relationship Diagram

Social system design: Feed

See the feed of questions with answers in story form with the list of categories in a sidebar to the left. There will also be a persistent top navigation throughout the whole site.

Social system design: Profile

The feed here will only be a feed of the user’s own posts. Above the feed, there’s a potential profile picture, your name, when you created your account (possibly), and how many posts you have made.

Social system design: List of categories (users)

Since all of our users are anonymous, we will map out a network of “categories.” The list to the left of the feed here is a list of categories that are strung together in a network, like a network of friends, but here, there are a network of posts posted by people under each category that are all connected. Here, this view shows when a user clicks on a category. You can see the category’s icon with the placeholder oval, the category name, a brief description of the category, and the number of posts. The feed here is only posts that fall under that category.
